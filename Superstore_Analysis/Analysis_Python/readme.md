3. Sales, Profit, Quantity and Discount Distribution:

- Many orders are sold with no discount or a 20% discount.

4. Outlier Analysis:

- Profit shows both high profits and heavy losses --> some orders perform very differently.

- Most discounts are low (0–20%), while very high discounts are given only in a small number of orders.


5. Relationship Analysis

<img width="1031" height="845" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6fecb998-f5b3-4252-bb40-75265be1e5bb" />

i. Sales vr Profit: 
- Positive relationship.
- Sales ^ - Profit ^
- Few high-sales orders result in loss --> some unprofitable transactions.
  
ii. Discount vs Profit:
- High discount reduces Profit.
- Orders with 50% Discount --> Low/Negative Profit.

iii. Quantity vr Profit:
- Weak relationship.
- Selling more units doesn't always lead to higher profit; profitability depends on factors like discount, product category, and margin.

iv. Sales vs Discount:
- Most orders have low sales and low discounts

<img width="456" height="365" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8ebf7595-8ddc-46e9-bf47-69eb11f19ab4" />

- Sales have strongest +ve relationship with Profit (0.48).
- Discount negatively impacts Profit (-0.22).
- Quantity has weak relationship with Sales (0.20) --> no relationship with Discount (0.0086).
- Managing discounts effectively is important for maintaining profitability.
